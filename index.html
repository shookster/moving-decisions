Special Education: Autism & ADHD Services

All three districts are federally required under IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act) to provide a free and appropriate public education (FAPE) to students with disabilities. The following summarizes district-specific programs relevant to students with ASD and ADHD.

Hopkins ISD 270 — Special Education

  • Provides IEP services for Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) at all school levels, including Federal Setting III programs for students requiring 60% or more of the school day in special education.[9]
  • Section 504 plans explicitly cover ADD/ADHD, learning disabilities, and autism. Plans are managed by school counselors and social workers at each building.[10]
  • Early Childhood Special Education (ECSE) begins at birth through age 5 at the Harley Hopkins Family Center and all elementary sites.
  • Serves students ages 2½ through 26 with IEPs across a full continuum of settings.
  • District has 7.99 school psychologists and 217.96 FTE instructional aides on staff.[11]

Cherokee County School District — Special Education

  • Maintains a dedicated Department of Special Education and Section 504, explicitly listing Autism (including Asperger's and Pervasive Developmental Disorders) and ADHD as covered eligibility categories.[12]
  • Provides services from birth through age 21 under IDEA; all referrals are confidential and provided at no cost.
  • Offers Extended School Year (ESY) services for eligible students whose skills regress during breaks.
  • IEP meetings are coordinated through each school's Special Education Facilitator; parents may contact the Department of Special Education at 770-704-4315 for concerns.[13]
  • Title VI Part B/IDEA funds support student identification aged 3–21 and IEP goal progress.[14]
  • Note: Georgia research indicates significant geographic variation in autism service providers statewide; Cherokee County's suburban Atlanta location provides relatively better access than rural Georgia.[15]

Inver Grove Heights ISD 199 — Special Education

  • PLUS / Transitions Programs serve students with mild to moderate cognitive delays and those with significant deficits in social skills, sensory motor regulation, and self-help skills — spanning elementary through high school.[16]
  • CARE / CID (Communication and Interaction Delays) Programs serve students with moderate to severe cognitive delays, medically fragile students, and those with multiple impairments — at Pine Bend Elementary, IGH Middle School, and Simley High School.[16]
  • Resource Programs (less than 60% of day in special education) are available at all district schools, K–12.
  • EBD (Emotional and Behavioral Disabilities) Programs serve students with significant behavioral and emotional concerns at all school levels.
  • Early Childhood Special Education (ECSE) classes available from birth through age 5.[17]
  • 74% of Simley teachers hold advanced degrees, compared to a state average of 56%.[18]

What This Means for Families

Best for: Safety & Community Consistency

Cherokee County / Creekview (GA) — All ZIP codes have extremely low violent crime relative to national averages, and the district's income is highly consistent across its three ZIPs. Families who prioritize physical safety and a stable, homogeneous community will find this district compelling. The tradeoff is limited racial diversity.

Best for: Wealth, Resources & Diversity

Hopkins ISD 270 (MN) — The district's top ZIP codes (Wayzata, Edina, Eden Prairie) are among the wealthiest and safest suburbs in the country. Hopkins High School itself is significantly racially diverse and majority-minority. The caveat: extreme income variation across 12 ZIP codes means your experience depends heavily on which ZIP you choose.

Best for: Diversity on a Budget

Inver Grove Heights ISD 199 (MN) — Simley is the most diverse of the three high schools by minority enrollment percentage, with a notably high Hispanic student population. Home values and incomes are consistent and more accessible than Hopkins' premium ZIPs. The tradeoffs are a higher student/teacher ratio and elevated (though still below-national-average) violent crime in parts of the city.

Best for: Students with ASD or ADHD

All three districts offer federally compliant IEP and 504 programs for autism and ADHD. Hopkins ISD 270 stands out for its comprehensive Federal Setting III programs and large support staff (218 instructional aides, 8 school psychologists). ISD 199 offers the most explicitly named continuum of ASD-adjacent programs (CARE/CID, PLUS/Transitions). Cherokee County CCSD covers both ASD and ADHD explicitly but has more variable access to external autism service providers depending on location within the county.

Property crime footnote: Property crime (theft, burglary, motor vehicle theft) is excluded from the safety rankings above per the scope of this report. Notably, St. Louis Park ZIPs (55416, 55426) in the Hopkins district have property crime rates approximately 77% above the national average, driven largely by commercial theft. Cherokee County ZIP codes have property crime rates well below national averages. Inver Grove Heights property crime runs approximately 7.7% above the national average. Families concerned about property crime should weight the Cherokee County district favorably on this dimension.
